## 1. What Diamond Camo Actually Is

CODM's completionist camo ladder runs, in order: **Common tier camos → Gold → Platinum → Damascus → Diamond**. Diamond sits above Damascus as a per-weapon mastery camo — it doesn't unlock account-wide, and it doesn't carry over to weapon blueprints (a blueprint is a cosmetic skin sitting on top of the base weapon; camo progress is tracked on the base weapon itself, and equipping Diamond on a blueprint just overrides its skin visually where the game allows it).

| Camo | Tier | What it requires |
|---|---|---|
| Gold | Mid | Complete all of a weapon's basic/standard camo challenges (headshots, hipfire, no-attachment kills, etc.) |
| Platinum | High | Get Gold on **every weapon in that class** (e.g. every AR) |
| Damascus | High | Get Gold on **every weapon in the entire game**, across all classes |
| **Diamond** | Mastery | Get **Gold on the individual weapon**, then grind class-specific match-kill quotas on that same weapon |

Diamond and Damascus are independent tracks that both sit above Gold — you don't need Damascus to start Diamond, and getting Diamond on one gun doesn't affect Platinum/Damascus progress on others. Diamond is purely a per-weapon, kills-in-a-match grind; Platinum/Damascus are about breadth (owning Gold across many weapons), while Diamond is about depth (repeated high performance on one weapon).

> **Note:** Requirement numbers below are widely documented and have been stable since Diamond was introduced in Season 13 (2020) through the current 2026 seasons, but Activision has occasionally adjusted specific quotas or added new weapon categories in later updates. Always cross-check the exact number shown in your own **Gunsmith → Camo → Completionist** tab before grinding, since that in-game panel is the only fully authoritative source for your current weapon roster and any season-specific overrides.

## 2. Core Mechanics

- **Gold is a hard prerequisite.** You cannot make Diamond progress on a weapon until that weapon's Gold camo is unlocked.
- **The core unit is "10 kills in one completed match."** For most classes, a match only counts toward Diamond progress if you get 10 kills with that specific weapon *and* the match runs to completion. You cannot quit right after hitting 10 kills and have it count — the match has to end normally.
- **Only weapon kills count.** Kills from scorestreaks, lethal/tactical equipment, and Operator Skills do **not** count toward a weapon's Diamond kill quota — the kill has to come from the gun itself.
- **Progress is per-weapon, not per-class.** Diamond on one AR does not give progress toward Diamond on another AR. Each weapon tracks its own count independently.
- **Deaths don't reset your match progress**, but they do cost you time — going 10-8 in a completed match still counts the same as going 10-0.
- **Leaving a match early forfeits post-match rewards** (and, per community consensus and Activision's own leaver-penalty policy, is treated as an incomplete/abandoned match for challenge purposes). If you're grinding a 10-kill quota, always finish the match.

## 3. Requirements by Weapon Class

These are the standard Diamond kill/match quotas, consistent across sources going back to the camo's introduction:

| Category | Requirement per weapon | Matches needed | Total kills per weapon |
|---|---|---|---|
| Assault Rifles | 10 kills/match | 150 | 1,500 |
| SMGs | 10 kills/match | 120 | 1,200 |
| LMGs | 10 kills/match | 120 | 1,200 |
| Sniper Rifles | 10 kills/match | 120 | 1,200 |
| Marksman Rifles | 10 kills/match | 120 (treated the same as sniper-class weapons in current Gunsmith categorization) | 1,200 |
| Shotguns | 10 kills/match | 120 | 1,200 |
| Pistols | 10 kills/match | 80 | 800 |
| Melee | No match requirement | — | 500 total kills, any number of matches |
| Launchers | No kill quota | — | 100 UAV takedowns per launcher |

> **Warning:** Assault Rifles have the single highest requirement in the game at 150 ten-kill matches. Budget the most time for this class, or grind it first while your motivation is highest.

> **Note on Marksman Rifles:** Some seasons fold Marksman Rifles into the Sniper category in Gunsmith, others list them separately with identical numbers. The kill logic is the same either way — 10 kills/match, 120 matches — but confirm which tab your specific weapon sits under in-game.

## 4. Recommended Grind Order

Ranked roughly easiest-to-hardest based on total time investment and how forgiving the weapon class is for consistently reaching 10 kills:

1. **Pistols** — lowest total requirement (800 kills, 80 matches), fast time-to-finish, good as secondary while grinding a primary simultaneously.
2. **SMGs** — high mobility, strong at close range on small maps, easy to string kills together in Hardpoint/Domination hotspots.
3. **Shotguns** — devastating on tight maps, but positioning-dependent; can be slower if you're not aggressive.
4. **LMGs** — strong sustained damage and large magazines mean fewer reloads mid-fight, but poor mobility can cost you kills in open areas.
5. **Sniper/Marksman Rifles** — rewarding on the right maps (long sightlines), but one-shot misses cost tempo; quickscoping mitigates this.
6. **Launchers** — no kill grind, but 100 UAV takedowns per launcher is a specific, situational objective that depends on enemies actually calling in UAVs.
7. **Melee** — 500 total kills sounds small, but melee range and lack of a hitscan weapon make it the slowest kills-per-minute class.
8. **Assault Rifles** — save for last or grind in parallel from day one; 150 matches is the largest total time commitment in the game.

**Practical tip:** run two loadout slots with different classes (e.g. an SMG primary and a pistol secondary) so every match generates progress on two weapons simultaneously, cutting your total grind time roughly in half.

## 5. Best Maps and Modes

### Maps
Small, high-traffic maps maximize kill density per minute, which is what the 10-kills-in-a-match requirement rewards.

| Map | Why it works |
|---|---|
| Shipment | Extremely small, constant close-range engagements, ideal for SMGs/shotguns/melee |
| Nuketown | Symmetric, fast rotations, good all-around map for ARs and SMGs |
| Rust | Vertical, tight sightlines, good for both close range and short-mid snipes |
| Killhouse | Compact, good for shotgun/SMG/melee grinds |
| Crossfire | Longer lanes, useful for AR/sniper longshot-adjacent grinding |
| Cage | Small vertical arena good for close-quarters classes |

Avoid large, open maps (e.g. Blackout-style large MP maps) when grinding SMG/shotgun/melee — travel time between fights eats into your 10-kill window before the match ends.

### Modes

| Mode | Best for | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Team Deathmatch (small maps) | Almost everything | Highest overall kill volume, straightforward objective |
| Hardpoint | ARs, SMGs, LMGs | Forces fights into one zone — very high kill density if you hold the hill |
| Domination | ARs, SMGs | Predictable fight lanes around flags |
| Kill Confirmed | Any class | Confirming kills doesn't add extra requirements, and death is less punishing since dying doesn't remove your existing kill count |
| Free-For-All | Pistols, snipers, melee | No teammates competing for kills, full credit for every elimination |
| Frontline | ARs, SMGs | Continuous respawns near the fight, similar density to TDM |

> **Tip:** Free-For-All is particularly strong for secondary/pistol and melee grinding because there's no team to "steal" kills, and low player counts mean more 1v1 opportunities.

## 6. Loadout & Gunsmith Philosophy

Rather than copying a specific build (which goes stale every balance patch), prioritize attachments by what the grind actually needs:

- **Headshot-heavy challenges (Gold prerequisite):** any optic that gives a clean, uncluttered sight picture at your preferred range, plus attachments that reduce flinch/recoil so your reticle stays on the head after the first shot.
- **Longshot challenges:** stability and range-boosting attachments (long barrels, stocks that reduce sway) matter more than fire rate.
- **Hipfire challenges:** laser attachments and hipfire-spread-reducing grips/stocks; sacrifice ADS speed for hipfire accuracy on these specific attempts.
- **General Diamond grinding (10 kills/match):** prioritize **time-to-kill and control** over raw stats — extended magazines reduce mid-fight reloads, and recoil-control attachments (compensators, angled grips) matter more for consistency than pure damage attachments once you're already using a meta weapon.
- **Perks:** something that speeds up your engagement loop (faster ADS, faster sprint-to-fire) generally helps more for volume-kill grinding than pure survivability perks, though a survivability perk (fast health regen, tougher against explosives) is worth running on maps/modes where you're expected to stay alive to close out a 10-kill match.

Because weapon balance shifts every season, check current tier-list/meta discussion for **Season 6: Take Your Heart** specifically before locking in a "best gun" — grind on a weapon that's currently strong, since a meta pick gets you to 10 kills faster and more consistently than a weak one.

## 7. Settings That Actually Help

Exact sensitivity numbers are highly personal — what works for a claw-grip 4-finger player on a tablet will feel wrong to someone playing 2-finger on a phone. Instead of copying someone else's numbers verbatim, tune around these principles:

- **Separate your camera and ADS-fire sensitivities.** A common competitive approach is running lower "standard" camera sensitivity for controlled tracking, with a moderately higher ADS/firing sensitivity so you can snap onto targets once you're aiming.
- **Reduce Kick Scale / camera recoil settings toward the lower end of the range** if your device supports it — this keeps your sight picture on target for longer bursts, which matters directly for 10-kill-in-a-match consistency.
- **Enable Gyroscope for fine aim correction** if you're on a phone/tablet with a supported gyro — most competitive players use it as a supplement to touch aim rather than a replacement, for that last bit of micro-correction onto the head.
- **Turn on "Advanced HUD"/enemy outline or target-highlight visual accessibility options** where available under Settings → HUD, and adjust color-blind/visibility filters — anything that makes enemy silhouettes pop against the map's color palette helps you react faster, which is a legitimate in-game accessibility feature rather than a hack.
- **Use Auto Sprint and Slide Behavior settings** that minimize input taps needed to move — fewer inputs spent on movement means more attention on aim during your kill window.
- **Test sensitivity changes in a private/custom match** before taking them into a real grind session — muscle memory takes a few matches to adjust, and you don't want to burn a 10-kill attempt while re-learning your own settings.

> **Warning:** Be skeptical of any "settings" tip that claims to remove recoil entirely or guarantees kills — recoil reduction settings adjust camera/gyro sensitivity curves, they don't change the weapon's actual recoil pattern, which is server-side.

## 8. Class-by-Class Notes

**Assault Rifles** — Largest grind (150 matches). Run Hardpoint or Domination on small-to-medium maps to guarantee consistent engagements; ARs' range flexibility makes them viable on more map types than SMGs, which helps hit 10 kills even on slightly larger maps.

**SMGs** — Fastest kill-per-minute class on close maps. Shipment/Nuketown/Killhouse TDM is close to optimal. Mobility perks pay off more here than on any other class.

**LMGs** — Slower mobility hurts on open maps; stick to chokepoint-heavy maps and hold angles rather than pushing. Large magazines mean you can often get your 10 kills without needing to reload mid-fight.

**Shotguns** — Extremely map-dependent; on tight maps they out-duel almost everything, on open maps they're nearly unusable. Stay on Shipment/Killhouse-style maps exclusively while grinding this class.

**Sniper/Marksman Rifles** — Quickscoping on medium-range maps tends to be faster for volume kills than pure hardscoping, since hardscoping requires holding angles and waiting. Crossfire and Rust-style maps with mixed sightlines work well.

**Pistols** — Best run as your primary focus in Free-For-All so you're not relying on a primary weapon to secure most kills; low total requirement (800) makes this a good "finish quickly" class.

**Launchers** — This isn't a kill grind, it's a UAV-denial grind. Play modes/maps where enemies frequently call in UAVs (objective modes tend to see more streak usage than pure TDM), and prioritize shooting down UAVs the instant they spawn.

**Melee** — Lowest total kill count (500) but the slowest per-minute, since you must close to point-blank range. Small maps, and picking fights against players already low on health (post-explosion, post-fall damage) meaningfully speeds this up. Movement (sliding into range) helps you close distance without exposing yourself to gunfire for as long.

## 9. Common Mistakes

- **Grinding Gold-tier challenges and Diamond quotas as if they're unrelated** — Gold has to be finished first, so half-finishing Gold across many weapons before starting any Diamond grind wastes time; finish Gold on your priority weapon completely, then move to Diamond immediately.
- **Quitting matches after reaching 10 kills** — the match must complete for the attempt to count; leaving early forfeits that match's progress.
- **Farming kills with scorestreaks or lethals** thinking they'll count toward the weapon's Diamond progress — they won't, only weapon kills count.
- **Grinding shotguns or melee on large, open maps** — both classes need short engagement distances to be efficient; using them on the wrong map type massively slows the grind.
- **Ignoring current meta shifts** — a weapon nerfed mid-season can make an in-progress grind noticeably slower; check patch notes before committing large amounts of time to one weapon.
- **Not running two weapons per match** — playing a primary and secondary from different classes doubles your progress-per-match at essentially no extra time cost.
- **Overtuning sensitivity right before a grind session** — settings changes need practice matches to adapt to; changing settings and immediately grinding hard matches often produces worse results, not better.

## 10. FAQ

**Do I need Gold before I can start Diamond on a weapon?**
Yes — Gold on that specific weapon is a hard prerequisite for Diamond progress to begin.

**Does Diamond camo transfer to a weapon's blueprints?**
Diamond progress is tracked on the base weapon. Where the game allows manual camo application to a blueprint, you can apply Diamond visually once it's unlocked on the base gun, but the blueprint doesn't earn or hold separate Diamond progress of its own.

**Do kills from scorestreaks or lethal equipment count toward Diamond?**
No — only kills scored with the weapon itself count.

**Does leaving a match early after getting 10 kills still count?**
No — the match needs to run to completion for that attempt to register.

**Does dying during the match reset my progress in that match?**
No — you just need to net 10 kills by match end; deaths don't remove kills already scored.

**Do Battle Royale matches count toward Diamond progress?**
Diamond's "10 kills in a match" quota is a Multiplayer-mode mechanic. Battle Royale still grants weapon XP toward Gold-tier challenges you're mid-progress on, but the class-specific Diamond match-kill quotas are earned in Multiplayer.

**Is Diamond the highest camo tier in the game?**
No — Damascus (Gold on every weapon in the game) and Aether-tier camos (typically tied to specific modes like Undead Siege/Zombies) sit above or alongside Diamond depending on the season; Diamond specifically is the per-weapon mastery track.

**Can I grind multiple weapons in the same match?**
Yes, as long as you switch between them — each weapon only earns progress from kills scored with that weapon while it's equipped.
